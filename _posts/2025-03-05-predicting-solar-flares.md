### What are Solar Flares?
Solar flares are sudden eruptions of energy from the sun that can cause disruptions to Earth's magnetic field and communication systems. Understanding and predicting solar flares is crucial for space weather forecasting and ensuring the safety of astronauts and satellites.

### What is a Solar Flare?
The sun is a star that emits light and heat. It is made up of plasma, which is a hot, ionized gas. The sun's surface is covered by a layer of plasma called the photosphere. There is a magnetic field in the sun's photosphere that is generated by the movement of the plasma. The earth also has a magnetic field because of the movement of the molten iron in the earth's core. On regular days, the magnetic fields of the sun and the earth cancel each other out. However, on days when the sun's magnetic field is facing the earth's magnetic field, the magnetic fields of the sun and the earth add up and cause a solar flare.

### How solar flares affect GPS data?
The magnetic field of the sun can affect the GPS data by causing errors in the time it takes for the GPS signal to travel from the satellite to the receiver. This is because the magnetic field of the sun can cause the GPS signal to travel slower or faster than it should.

So, if we can predict the solar flares, we can predict the errors in the GPS data and we can use that to improve the accuracy of the GPS data.
